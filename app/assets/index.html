frequently asked questions

Why Walla Walla? Why Now?

Everyone who has spent time in the Walla Walla Valley knows this is a special place blessed with an abundance of natural beauty and a history of supporting bold ideas. We think it's only natural that this town should foster a creative community befitting its splendor.

We want to prove that Walla Walla can support a vibrant, sustainable, and renowned community of independent artists. We hope you'll join us in our experiment.

Why popUP?

Being a pop-up gallery means we don't have a permanent lease, and are unlikely to mount a show in the same place twice. This first show will only be up for 3 weeks, then we pack up and go... until the next show!

We think this is the best business model for a co-operative gallery in Walla Walla, right now. Our operation is collectively financed by all members, and we cannot afford to keep the lights on all year in an expensive downtown property. The limited scope of the work involved also allows us to keep our day jobs.

By leasing property one month at a time, we gain flexibility and occupy empty retail spaces which would otherwise remain vacant. We have found that property owners are glad to cut us a good deal, since in addition to rent we are providing them with excellent visibility for a space they would like to rent out.

If you are interested in offering your retail space for a future show, please contact us at galery@wallapopup.com.

Who are you?

popUP was organized by Lynn Woolson and Ben Lerchin, both artists who live and work in Walla Walla.

Our first exhibition represents a diverse group of artists at various stages in their careers and working with a wide variety of media. However we all have one thing in common: we are very excited to bring our respective talents to bear in developing Walla Walla's creative community.
